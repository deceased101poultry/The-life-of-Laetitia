For the Life of Laetitia by Merle Hodge, tells the story of 12-year-old Laetitia, who is forced to leave her comfortable life with her grandparents and move to the city to live with her father, Orville Cephas. The novel explores themes of social class, racism, and the importance of education, particularly for women, in the Caribbean. Laetitia's journey highlights the challenges she faces in adjusting to a new environment, dealing with her abusive father, and navigating the prejudices she encounters at school.
